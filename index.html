Electoral Systems Compared:
Look back at 2017 BC Elections

Wonder what BC Government would look like under different electoral systems? Then read on.

What's this about?

Between October 22nd and November 30th British Columbians will vote in a mail-in referendum on the Electoral Reform. Aside from the status quo, three electoral systems are presented as options. These three options aim to create a government that proportionally represents BC voters as a whole: 40% of popular vote for a party should mean party getting 40% of seats. While the options seem complicated, the aim of this site is to show how 2017 BC election would unfold in all three cases.

First Past The Post

Each electoral district elects a single MLA based on highest number of votes. This MLA represents the population of the district in the Legislative Assembly.

Dual Member Proportional

Most existing electoral districts are combined to have 2 MLA seats. One is elected based on FPTP, the other is allocated to a party in order to achieve proportional representation accross BC.

Mixed Member Proportional

Existing districts are combined into larger districts with 1 MLA seat. Districts are grouped into regions with additional seats assigned to each. District elects one MLA based on FPTP, while regional seats are allocated to parties based on popular vote.

Rural-Urban Proportional

Existing rural districts will be converted to use MMP system. Existing (semi)urban districts will consolidate into larger districts. These will elect a group of MLAs based on a ranked ballot, likely resulting in proportional representation in the district.
